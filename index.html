The idea for a drama troupe was born in the minds of a few of the Simla librarians in 2009...

Mission:

To provide a program of educational, fun experiences in all aspects of theatre for youth and adults.

Goals:

  • Present fun family entertainment to the rural Elbert County and rural El Paso County communities in a theatrical venue.
  • Create opportunities for children and adults to develop talents in all aspects of theatre arts
  • Provide an outlet for creative community involvement in a non-exclusionary company that lets everyone who’s ever dreamed of being on a stage realize their dream
  • Bring a strong desire to teach theatre arts to anyone with a desire to learn
  • Provide free theatrical enjoyment to our rural community
  • Develop and showcase local talent to the maximum extent possible

In 2009 the troupe performed their first play which was “The Dollar” by David Pinski

Plot: The play takes place in the rural countryside in 1920 when a group of (not so good) shakespearian actors are traveling to their next performance when they stumble upon a dollar that quickly turns them against each other.

The first performance went better than expected, but during the second performance one of the actors skipped to a line in the play excluding 1/3 of the play, and the performers went with it trying not to bust up laughing. It turned out well, as they were this particular performance was in front of Alzheimer's patients.

In 2010 the troupe began work on their next 2 plays which they did as a back to back performance. The first was a spoof on the story of Pinocchio.

Plot: The story takes place in Geptto’s workshop when the fairy Modmother (yes it’s spelled like that on purpose) gives Geppeto a choice between turning either a boy or girl doll into a real person. The Modmother makes both Pinocchio and a Shirley Temple doll alive. Geppeto then lives with both of them for a week not being able to choose between them he asks the Modmother to maker them both real which caused unexpected results.

During one performance the entire cast watched in horror as the tiny Jiminy Cricket fell from Pinoccio’s shoulder and shattered into pieces on the stage, and then had to stiffle their laughter and continue on with the play.

The second play was a spoof on the story of Snow White.

Plot: This one mostly follows the main story with a few small changes. Due not having any apples on hand at the time the evil Queen is forced to us a poisoned banana instead, and in a strange turn of events the woodcutter isn’t who he seems to be and the Queen turns good and falls in love with the dwarf known as Doc.

For a change the performance went as expected.

In 2011 the troupe worked on their next spoof which was based on the story of Hansel and Gretel

Plot: In this version, Hansel is so whinny one almost wishes the witch would eat him just to get some quiet. Add in a confused fairy godmother looking for Cinderella, and a Hansel and Gretel walking off with the deed to the ginger bread house, and a spoof is born.

In one scene the actors were supposed to eat candy off of the cardboard prop house. But they happened to choose very chewy candy, so one of the actors ended up swallowing 2 starbursts whole, and had to do his lines around a mint in his mouth.

In 2012 The troupe had some trouble recruiting actors, so they did some readers theatre and interactive plays for their audiences. These went fairly well, except when they performed at the local retirement home one of the residents came up and tried to read the actor’s shirt. She also kept telling one of the other actors that she was very beautiful. To her credit, she said thank you and continued on with her lines
